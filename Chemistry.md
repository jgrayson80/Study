# Chemistry

## General Chemistry

### Carbohydrates

1. Biochemical theory and physiology
   1. Metabolic pathways
      1. Glucose - Converted to glucose-6-phosphate using ATP and hexokinase
         1. (Glycolysis) Embden-Meyerhof
            1. Tricarboxylic acid cycle (Aerobic)
               1. Produces Acetyl-coenzyme A
            2. Glycolysis (Anaerobic)
               1. Produces Lactic acid
         2. Hexose monophosphate
            1. Produces NADPH
         3. Glycogenesis/glycogenolysis
            1. Helps regulate blood glucose level
         4. Gluconeogenesis
            1. Formation of glucose from noncarbohydrates
   2. Normal and abnormal states
      1. Hyperglycemia
         1. Diabetes mellitus - deficiency of insulin secretion or action
            1. Type 1 - Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic islet beta cells
            2. Type 2 - Insulin resistance
         2. Other
            1. Genetic defects
               1. Beta cell function
               2. Insulin receptor
               3. Other still
            2. Pancreatic or endocrine disease
            3. Drug or chemical induced
         3. Gestational diabetes mellitus
      2. Hypoglycemia
         1. Reactive
         2. Spontaneous
         3. Glycogen storage diseases
            1. 10 types associated with enzyme deficiency
               1. Type I (von Gierke's) - most common, is Glucose-6-phosphate deficiency
   3. Physical and chemical properties
      1. Classification
         1. Monosaccharides - 1 unit
            1. Glucose
            2. Fructose
            3. Galactose
         2. Oligosaccharides - 2 to 10 units
            1. Disaccharides
               1. Maltose
               2. Lactose
               3. Sucrose
         3. Polysaccharides - >10 units
            1. Starch
            2. Glycogen
      2. Stereoisomers
      3. Chemical properties
         1. Reducing substances - contains ketone or aldehyde group
         2. Glycosidic bonding
2. Test procedures
   1. Principles
      1. Random plasma glucose
      2. Fasting plasma glucose
      3. Urine glucose - used as screening test - glucose appears in urine when blood glucose level exceeds the renal threshold
      4. Two-hour postprandial plasma glucose - plasma glucose measured after patient is given a standard load of glucose
      5. Oral glucose tolerance test
      6. Screening test for gestational diabetes mellitus
      7. Intravenous glucose tolerance test
      8. Self-monitoring
      9.  Urine microalbumin - Detects damage to kidneys due to diabetic complications
      10. Serum and urine ketone - degree of ketoacidosis
      11. Autoantibodies
      12. Five-hour glucose tolerance test - hypoglycemia
      13. Insulin - pancreatic cancer
      14. C-peptide - measures endogenous insulin secretion
      15. Insulin tolerance
      16. Tolbutamide tolerance - stimulates pancrease to secrete insulin
      17. Epinepherine tolerance - stimulates breakdown of glycogen
     
   2. Special precautions, specimen collection and processing, toubleshooting, and interfering substances
   3. Tolerance testing
   4. Glycated proteins
3. Test result interpretation
4. Disease state correlation
